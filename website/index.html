Models4PT — Clinically Relevant Causal Diagrams

Models4PT is built on the foundation of the DAGitty project, developed by Johannes Textor, to address clinical and research needs in physical therapy and related fields. For more on the original project, visit dagitty.net.

Models4PT provides an intuitive browser-based environment for creating, editing, and analyzing causal diagrams. It builds upon the robust features of DAGitty while introducing enhancements specifically tailored for clinical reasoning and research in physical therapy and related healthcare fields. By leveraging critical realism principles, Models4PT incorporates deeper causal structures into modeling, allowing researchers to:

  • Identify and address sources of bias in empirical studies more effectively.
  • Represent domains of causality (empirical, actual, real) for a more nuanced understanding of clinical phenomena (in development).
  • Integrate nested and hierarchical submodels to reflect complex mechanisms in healthcare (in development).

By adopting the user-friendly interface from DAGitty and focusing on PT applications, Models4PT bridges the gap between theoretical frameworks and real-world research and clinical needs, empowering clinicians and researchers to design and analyze models that reflect the multifaceted nature of clinical practice through clinical inquiry.
