Models4PT

An interactive framework for **neuromechanical modeling, Bayesian inference, and causal analysis** in movement science.

**Bridging population statistics and individual decision-making through a critical realist approach.**

Why Models4PT?

Evidence-based practice relies heavily on population statistics, but individual cases demand **mechanism-based reasoning**. Models4PT integrates **neuromechanical modeling, causal inference, and Bayesian decision-making** into a single platform, allowing clinicians and researchers to explore **how dysfunction propagates and how evidence should inform decisions.**

The Three Modes of Inquiry

1️⃣ Neuromechanical Modeling

Interactive **Cytoscape.js-based networks** visualize muscle synergies, joint stability, and movement constraints.

Use Cases: Movement dysfunction analysis, regional interdependence, motor control adaptations.

2️⃣ Causal Analysis (DAGs & Directed Graphs)

**DAG-based models** clarify whether movement impairments are **correlations or causal factors.** Identify confounders, mediators, and effect modifiers in movement dysfunction research.

Use Cases: Investigating low back pain mechanisms, analyzing SFMA dysfunctions.

3️⃣ Bayesian Inference & Clinical Decision-Making

Move beyond static "evidence-based" claims—**apply probabilistic reasoning to real cases.** Bayesian modeling personalizes **decision-making by integrating both prior knowledge and patient-specific data.**

Use Cases: Predicting movement compensations, refining clinical assessments.
